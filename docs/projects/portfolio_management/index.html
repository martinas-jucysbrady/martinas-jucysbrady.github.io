Initial Portfolio

For my initial portfolio my main target was to diversify assets and minimalise any correlation that exists. I started off by choosing 32 assets from varying sectors and industries and took their stock price on a quarterly basis for 5 years starting from 19th May 2025, as I feel like this is more appropriate to meet a balance between the overall portfolio which is due in 2 months and a portfolio used for long term gains. Through more research I began to remove certain assets that had high correlation with multiple other assets and also those that I did not see as a worthwhile risk.

A few issues I ran into were adapting the data to include companies that were less than 5 years old, and that I had to adjust the risk-free rate in order to find the proper Sharpe Ratio.

These are the asset classes and their respective portfolio weight -

  • Bonds 15% ( BNDX was included to reduce volatility and have a “stable” growth.)

  • ETF 25% (VOO is the largest holding for this asset class as it allows direct access to the 500 largest companies in the US – which is heavily weighted by companies within the Technology sector.)

  • Stocks 30% (This asset class includes a variety of different assets from different sectors, I decided to not include any stocks from companies whose primary sector is Technology as I feel that the inclusion of VOO already gives enough exposure to this uncertain market. I also decided against including the likes of JNJ and PFE due to the current uncertainty with the U.S.’s policies over the Healthcare industry.)

  • Cryptocurrency 6% ( Although I avoided some risks within the market at the moment, and that BTC does have an extremely high volatility, I did decide to allocate a small percentage of my portfolio to cryptocurrency – BTC in particular – as a high-risk high-reward asset.)

  • REIT 10% ( similarly to BNDX, REITs can protect the portfolio against inflation but there is an extra amount of risk involved as REITs can be more volatile, and changes can happen very quickly.)

  • Cash ($, £, €) 14% (In case of any sudden future opportunities and to maintain a liquid asset. Small exposure to the EUR and GBP as some assets within the portfolio are traded in these currencies and relatively low risk.)

I’m not fully sure about the allocations at the moment, Sharpe Ratio of 0.61 indicates that a lot of improvement can be made. When I’ve used the solve function in Excel I’ve noticed that it tends to heavily weigh a portfolio on 1-2 assets so I’ve had to experiment a lot with the function and try to give sensible allocations to specific assets.

I selected my initial shortlist of assets by a mix of random and personal choices, and with the aim of having exposure to a large amount of sectors.

To narrow down this shortlist of assets I then made a correlation matrix and removed assets that were highly correlated with others (for example, I found that companies such as Amazon and Nvidia has very high correlation with other assets). I also tried to ensure that certain sectors were not too heavily weighted in the portfolio (hence I avoided using securities within the Technology sector as VOO is already heavily dominated by the technology sector and gives me sufficient exposure - I calculated that my portfolio is still ~22% weighted by technology sector securities right now).

I also decided that it may be best not to include some stocks as there was simply not enough information about them, or they are present in sectors that are currently going through a lot of change within the U.S. I also included some assets like BTC to ensure that I at least have a small exposure to the cryptocurrency market if it were to gain more traction (possibly if the U.S. were to implement more open usage of it - unlikely but at this moment it seems like anything could be possible due to the heightened unpredictability of the market).

Regarding my risk and reward objectives, my realistic goal would be for annualised returns of ~8%, and maintaining standard deviation of below 10%. In my roughwork and calculation for the Variance-Covariance Matrix I used Quarterly data and not annual.

From my understanding the Sharpe ratio will change by quite a bit if I were to change this data into Annual data. From using solver I noticed that it would give very unrealistic allocations to high volatility stocks that I personally wouldn’t be willing to risk so much on (e.g. TSLA, NVDA), so I had to adjust parts of the Solver function and set realistic limits as to how much I would put into each Asset class and sector.

My current Sharpe ratio based on Quarterly calculations is 0.61, but now I have tested it using Annual and have received a Sharpe ratio of 1.34, Expected Return of 17.81% and a Standard Deviation of 12.58%. From this I gather that I am exceeding my initial objectives for SR and ER but not SD.

From revising over my portfolio it may be that BTC is too heavily weighted and I may need to lower the portfolio weight of it.
